# What is HederaNet?

## The Problem: Infrastructure Gaps in Africa

Sub-Saharan Africa is home to over 1.2 billion people, yet nearly 700 million of them lack reliable access to electricity and roughly 800 million are unconnected to the internet. Traditional telecommunications companies and power utilities have largely failed to serve rural and peri-urban communities because the upfront infrastructure cost is high relative to the expected revenue. Communities wait decades for services that richer urban areas take for granted.

At the same time, the continent has abundant solar energy potential and a rapidly growing base of mobile phone users. The ingredients for community-owned infrastructure exist — what has been missing is a trusted, transparent coordination layer that lets community members pool resources, invest together, and share the resulting revenue fairly.

## The Solution: Community-Owned DePIN

HederaNet is a Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Network (DePIN). In a DePIN, the infrastructure — WiFi hotspots, solar panels, compute nodes — is owned and operated by community members rather than a central corporation. Those operators earn cryptocurrency rewards for every unit of service they provide, creating a direct economic incentive to deploy and maintain quality infrastructure.

HederaNet brings DePIN to Africa by combining three pillars:

1. **Decentralized Internet** — Anyone can buy a WiFi hotspot device, register it on HederaNet, and start earning HBAR every time a subscriber pays for internet access. Coverage gaps become profit opportunities.

2. **Peer-to-Peer Energy** — Solar panel owners register their installations, list excess energy on the energy market, and earn HBAR when neighbors buy kilowatt-hours from them. Energy buyers get clean, local electricity at competitive prices.

3. **Community Governance** — No single company controls HederaNet. Token holders propose and vote on every major decision — fee structures, reward rates, supported regions, new features — through an on-chain governance system.

## Why Hedera Hashgraph?

HederaNet is built on Hedera Hashgraph, not on Ethereum or any Proof-of-Work blockchain. This choice is deliberate:

### Speed
Hedera achieves consensus in 3 to 5 seconds, which means a subscriber's internet subscription activates nearly instantly after payment. A farmer buying 10 kWh of solar energy from a neighbor does not wait minutes for a block to confirm — the transaction is final in seconds.

### Cost
The average Hedera transaction costs roughly $0.0001. This makes micropayments viable — an operator can receive dozens of small subscription payments per day without fees eating into earnings. Traditional blockchains charge far more, often making transactions under $1 economically irrational.

### Environmental Impact
Hedera operates on a Proof-of-Stake consensus mechanism that uses a tiny fraction of the energy consumed by Proof-of-Work networks. Hedera has also committed to being carbon-negative, purchasing carbon offsets that exceed its energy consumption. For a platform built around renewable energy, this alignment is not cosmetic — it is core to the product's credibility.

### Enterprise Stability
The Hedera Governing Council includes Google, IBM, Boeing, Deutsche Telekom, and other global enterprises. This provides regulatory legitimacy and long-term network stability that newer blockchain projects cannot match, which matters enormously for the communities and institutions HederaNet serves.

## Who Benefits?

**Community operators** turn idle land, rooftops, and capital into income-generating infrastructure. A farmer with a solar panel can list surplus electricity and earn HBAR without knowing anything about cryptocurrency — the platform handles all the complexity.

**Subscribers** get access to affordable, locally-owned internet and clean energy. Because operators are community members rather than distant corporations, there is a human accountability layer that traditional utilities lack.

**Governance participants** — anyone holding HNET tokens — have a direct voice in how the network evolves. This is not advisory input; votes are executed on-chain and the outcome is binding.

**Africa broadly** gains a template for community-owned infrastructure that can scale from a single village to an entire continent, with every operator's performance permanently and transparently recorded on Hedera's public ledger.
